Mac & Cheese Casserole

Description

This Mac & cheese casserole is easily one of the best options to feed a large
group of people with a tried and true favorite! This recipe is designed to serve
six people, but can be adjusted to however many people! Overall, this recipe
provides a quick and easy solution for when the family surprise visits, the kids need
a quick meal, or any sitaution where delicious food is needed!

Ingredients

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni or tubular pasta or choice
  • 1/2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp of unsalted butter
  • 1/5 cup of all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of heavy whipping cream
  • 2 cups of sharp cheddar cheese shredded
  • 1 cup of Gruyere cheese or mozzarella if desired
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3/4 cups of panko crumbs panko preferred for maximum crunch!
  • 2 tbsp butter melted
  • 1/4 cup of Parmesan cheese shredded
  • Smoked paprika just a pinch! (1/10 of a tsp)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350F
  2. Lightly grease an apropriately sized baking dish
  3. Combine shredded cheeses in a bowl and set aside
  4. Cook the paste one minute less than al dente cooking time on box.
    Remove from heat, drain, and place in large bowl
  5. Drizzle olive oil over pasta and stir. Set aside
  6. Melt butter in a deep saucepan, dutch oven, or stock pot
  7. Whisk in flour over medium heat for ~1 minute or until
    bubbly and golden
  8. Gradually add and whisk milk and heavy cream until smooth.
    Whisk until bubbles surface and then 2 minutes afterwards
  9. Whisk in salt and pepper
  10. Add 2/3 of shredded cheese and whisk until smooth and whisk until
    creamy and smooth
  11. Stir in the cooled pasta until fully coated with cheese
  12. Pour half the mac and cheese into baking dish top with remaining
    shredded cheese
  13. In seperate bowl combine panko crumbs, parmesan cheese, melted
    butter, and paprika and sprinkle mixture over top of mac and cheese
  14. Bake in oven until golden brown (~30 min)
  15. Serve while its hot and enjoy!
